Overall Description

Everyone carries a phone in today’s world. Our phones hold with them information about us that we need. In many cases our phones know us better than we know ourselves. Therefore, it is important to keep our devices secured so that we are the only ones who can unlock them.

Traditional methods of unlocking phones with passwords and bio detectors like face and finger scans have kept us safe. However, these methods of security could use a creative update!

Our group wants to introduce two new ways to unlock our phones. The first unlocking technique is based on gesture input: as patterns light up buttons the user must watch for their secret pattern to be displayed and then repeat that pattern correctly to unlock. The second unlock technique is based on phone sensor data: as the user must interact with the phone’s camera as another person’s hand and act out their secret handshake correctly to unlock.

 

Write-up for the concept sketches

The concept sketches labelled “Project 2 Concept Sketches: touch based” are sketches of ways to unlock a phone based on touch phone gestures. These ideas are based on research about what the touch screen input can be interpreted as. For example, a quick touch can be a button press or a dragging a finger can be a swipe. Different actions can validate different user input to act as security. Each sketch transcribes a different interaction on the screen with a finger to hold different information as a secret so that only the correct person with the right knowledge can unlock it. Out of all these sketches our group ended up going with the memory game button (sketch 1) as the first idea to evolve it into our final project hand in. We went with this idea of its novel and creative take on the traditional press input. It is a fun way to effectively maintain security. My contribution to the project was that I came up with the initial idea in its basic form.

The sketches labelled “Project 2 Concept Sketches: sensor” are sketches of ways to unlock phones based on device sensors. These ideas are based on the in class and lab code snippets that showcase the ability of phone sensors in different ways. In coming up with these concepts we thought about the security around how sensors collect different information and how that information could be used to validate the owner of a phone. Out of all these sketches our group chose the secret handshake idea to continue developing and implementing. We thought this was a creative and unique interaction scenario that could be adapted to a phone interaction to unlock. We saw opportunities in utilizing different sensors in technical aspects to convey this cool concept. My contribution to this idea is sketching up the initial concept and developing the base functionality through code and trailing the machine learning model.

The sketches labelled “Project 2 Detail sketches: sensor” are iterations on the secret handshake unlock. The idea around these concepts were ways to utilize different sensors to recognize a handshake pattern. Some sketches apply the gyroscope feature on the phone to recognize shaking actions and others use the camera. In the end the group ended up with the idea to take advantage of the camera (sketch 5) because we found that the gyroscope sensors were too limiting because it could not validate actions such as high fives and fist bumps without also using inputs alongside the sensors. The pop up emojis surrounding the camera frame add animation to provide feedback that the system is recognizing the action the user is taking. With this combination of animation and sensor input utilization we were able to implement the creative ability to unlock the device with a combination of friendly hand gestures that simulated a real world way of greeting others. I contributed by training the AI model and coding the base functionality (about 60%).
